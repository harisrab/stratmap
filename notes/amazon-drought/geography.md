## Geography and map logic

In much of the Amazon, the river is the road. That makes low water a transportation problem first, and a public health or food-security problem soon after.


### Manaus as the logistics nerve center

Manaus anchors regional shipping, river gauges, and emergency planning. Conditions there became the most visible signal of the drought's severity.


### Lake Tefé and ecological stress

The crisis was not only about vessel draft. Heat and low water contributed to ecological and public health emergencies in lake and tributary systems.


### River channels as fragile access corridors

Supply routes depend on navigable channels. As water falls, some corridors become too shallow for larger vessels, turning routine travel into a relief operation.
