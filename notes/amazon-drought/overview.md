> A climate and access notebook on low river levels near Manaus, isolated river communities, emergency dredging, and how drought turned the Amazon's waterways into a logistics emergency.

## Why this notebook works

This notebook treats the Amazon drought as an access crisis. Falling water levels were not only an environmental story; they affected fuel, food, medicine, and passenger movement for communities that rely on rivers as their main transport network.

Move through the event list from falling river levels to record lows, emergency dredging, fish die-offs, and the scramble to maintain supply lines. The actor list keeps port logistics, communities, emergency response, and health or environmental concerns distinct so the notebook can support both journalists and policy readers.
