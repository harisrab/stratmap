## Timeline and turning points

This notebook carries scenario events so you can scrub the time bar, jump between beats,
and compare how actors and geography shift together.


### River levels enter a worrying decline
**June 2023**

By early dry season, low water had become a visible operational concern for river transport and communities dependent on regular boat service.


### Transport disruptions spread through the river network
**September 2023**

As channels shallowed, communities saw more delays, smaller loads, and less reliable movement of fuel, food, and medicine.


### Rio Negro reaches record low levels near Manaus
**16 October 2023**

The record low crystallized the crisis and confirmed that the drought had become severe enough to alter routine logistics across the region.


### Fish die-offs signal ecological emergency at Lake Tefé
**Late September 2023**

The drought's ecological effects became impossible to ignore as extreme conditions around Lake Tefé produced major fish mortality and alarm over wider environmental stress.


### Emergency dredging and aid delivery intensify
**Late October 2023**

Authorities and responders pushed to keep channels usable enough for essential goods and emergency movement as low water persisted.


### Conditions begin to improve but vulnerability remains
**Late 2023**

Rains and higher water later eased the immediate crisis, yet the drought showed how quickly low water can turn the river system into a regional access emergency.
