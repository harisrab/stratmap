> A Cold War city sustained by air corridors, airfield throughput, and political resolve after the Soviet blockade cut land access to West Berlin.

## Why this notebook works

The Berlin Airlift is a good example of why historical notebooks benefit from geographic structure. The core story is not simply that aircraft flew into Berlin; it is that a besieged city could only be sustained if corridors, runway turnover, fuel, coal, food, and weather all stayed within tolerable limits.

This notebook helps history and policy readers compare political pressure with physical throughput. The event list explains the escalation from blockade to sustained lift, while the map emphasizes that Tempelhof, Gatow, Tegel, and the air corridors were the real operating system of West Berlin's survival.
