## Geography and map logic

The crisis depended on the short distance between Cuba and the continental United States, the location of launch complexes inside Cuba, and the maritime band where U.S. quarantine forces would try to regulate Soviet shipping without triggering general war.


### Missile sites and warning time

The central strategic shock was geographic: medium- and intermediate-range missiles in Cuba seemed to compress warning time and place the U.S. homeland inside a different threat envelope.


### The quarantine line as controlled coercion

The quarantine was a deliberately measured use of geography. It placed pressure at sea while still leaving room for communication and de-escalation.


### Guantanamo and Florida as flank references

Guantanamo Bay, South Florida, and the Florida Straits formed a broader operating picture around the core missile-site problem.
