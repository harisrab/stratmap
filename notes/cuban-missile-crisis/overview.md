> A notebook on missile deployment, naval quarantine, and the compressed geography that made October 1962 a global nuclear crisis.

## Why this notebook works

This notebook frames the Cuban Missile Crisis as a contest over time and distance. Missile sites in Cuba changed the perceived warning time for the United States, while the quarantine line at sea created a visible threshold where military pressure and diplomatic signaling met.

The map is most useful when paired with the timeline. Move from the U-2 discovery of missile sites to the quarantine, then to Black Saturday and the negotiated settlement. The forces panel keeps the Soviet, American, and Cuban roles distinct so the crisis does not collapse into a single U.S.-Soviet abstraction.
