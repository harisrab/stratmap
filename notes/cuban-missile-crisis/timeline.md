## Timeline and turning points

This notebook carries scenario events so you can scrub the time bar, jump between beats,
and compare how actors and geography shift together.


### U-2 imagery confirms missile deployment
**14 October 1962**

Reconnaissance photography revealed Soviet missile sites in Cuba, giving the U.S. leadership a concrete map of the emerging strategic threat.


### Kennedy announces the quarantine
**22 October 1962**

The televised address translated intelligence and policy into a public geographic frame: offensive weapons in Cuba and a quarantine line designed to block further escalation.


### The quarantine line goes active
**24 October 1962**

With U.S. naval forces in position, the crisis moved from intelligence and speech into direct physical enforcement at sea.


### Black Saturday pushes the crisis to the edge
**27 October 1962**

The shootdown of a U-2 over Cuba and other simultaneous pressures made 27 October the most dangerous day of the crisis.


### Khrushchev accepts a settlement
**28 October 1962**

Moscow agreed to remove the missiles from Cuba, allowing the crisis to shift from immediate confrontation to verification and rollback.


### The quarantine is lifted
**20 November 1962**

With bombers and key offensive systems removed, the United States lifted the quarantine and the crisis moved into its post-mortem phase.
