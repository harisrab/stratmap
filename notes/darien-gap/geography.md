## Geography and map logic

The Darién route compresses a regional migration story into a physically punishing corridor. Boats, footpaths, river crossings, and reception camps all create distinct spatial stages in the movement north.


### Embarkation on the Colombian side

Towns on the Gulf of Urabá became gateways into the crossing. That upstream geography matters because it shapes departure timing, smuggling pricing, and congestion before people even enter the jungle.


### Bajo Chiquito as the first relief hinge

The first Panamanian communities reached after the crossing function as triage points where exhaustion, injury, and information gaps become immediately visible.


### Lajas Blancas as a reception bottleneck

Panama's reception system turns the jungle route into a camp and transport management problem. Arrival volume and onward movement determine whether the system absorbs or jams.
