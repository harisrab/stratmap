> A corridor notebook on migrant movement through the Darién, reception bottlenecks in Panama, and the geography that turns a remote jungle route into a major hemispheric story.

## Why this notebook works

This notebook treats the Darién as a geography story first. The route is not just a line between Colombia and Panama; it is a sequence of embarkation points, jungle trail segments, river crossings, reception sites, and onward transport nodes. That chain is what makes the crisis legible for reporters and analysts.

Use the map to compare route logic with human bottlenecks. The event sequence highlights how seasonal surges, record crossing counts, and new enforcement measures changed the corridor. The forces panel frames the people and organizations involved without pretending that the notebook is only about state actors.
