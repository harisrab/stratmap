## Timeline and turning points

This notebook carries scenario events so you can scrub the time bar, jump between beats,
and compare how actors and geography shift together.


### Crossings accelerate above prior-year pace
**Early 2023**

The corridor entered 2023 with higher baseline movement, setting the stage for a year in which previous annual records would be overtaken.


### Seasonal conditions compound route risks
**Mid-2023 rainy season**

Heavy rain and terrain stress made the route even more dangerous while reception systems continued to absorb high numbers of arrivals.


### Annual crossing count surpasses the 2022 record
**September 2023**

By early autumn, the route had already exceeded the previous year's total, confirming that the movement was no longer episodic but structurally large.


### Crossings top half a million for the year
**December 2023**

The corridor closed 2023 as one of the hemisphere's defining migration routes, with more than 500,000 crossings recorded.


### New control measures alter the corridor conversation
**Spring 2024**

Panama and regional governments explored ways to reduce or redirect the flow, adding a policy layer to what had previously been read mostly as a humanitarian and reporting story.


### Reception bottlenecks remain the key operational question
**Mid-2024**

Even as route conditions and policy choices changed, the practical test remained whether eastern Panama's reception system could process arrivals safely and quickly.
