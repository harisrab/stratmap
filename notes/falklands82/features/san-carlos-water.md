# San Carlos Water

A constricted landing area that gave the British a workable amphibious entry point while still exposing ships to intense air attack.

This is where geography turned tactical. The same tight water that offered some shelter also packed ships, helicopters, and landing craft close together, making air defense and unloading tempo inseparable problems.
