> An expeditionary campaign where maritime exclusion zones, carrier aviation, amphibious landings, and the final approach to Stanley all depended on geography.

## Why this notebook works

This example shows how a strategic notebook can hold the full shape of a campaign instead of only a force tree. The story begins with Argentina's seizure of the islands, expands into the British effort to build a task force at range, and narrows again around a handful of decisive places: [Ascension Island](stratmap://map?lat=-7.9467&lon=-14.3559&zoom=8), the [Total Exclusion Zone](stratmap://map?lat=-51.8&lon=-59.0&zoom=7), [San Carlos Water](stratmap://map?lat=-51.566&lon=-59.08&zoom=11), [Goose Green](stratmap://map?lat=-51.8228&lon=-58.9792&zoom=11), and the final ring around [Port Stanley](stratmap://map?lat=-51.6938&lon=-57.8517&zoom=11).

It is useful for defense readers because the same canvas links actors, map notes, and timed events. You can open the forces tree to inspect the expeditionary task force, click the key geographic features for contextual notes, jump the map to [Ascension Island](stratmap://map?lat=-7.9467&lon=-14.3559&zoom=8) or [San Carlos Water](stratmap://map?lat=-51.566&lon=-59.08&zoom=11), and then step through the event list to see how the campaign accelerated once the British turned sea access into a landing opportunity.
