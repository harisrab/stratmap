## Timeline and turning points

This notebook carries scenario events so you can scrub the time bar, jump between beats,
and compare how actors and geography shift together.

### Argentina seizes the Falklands

**2 April 1982**

Argentine forces landed and took control of the islands, forcing London to decide whether it could assemble and sustain a recovery campaign across the South Atlantic. Jump to [Port Stanley](stratmap://map?lat=-51.6938&lon=-57.8517&zoom=11) to see the political center of gravity.

### Britain declares a maritime exclusion zone

**12 April 1982**

The exclusion zone was a military and political signal. It narrowed the legal and operational space around the islands and set the stage for submarine-led pressure on Argentine naval movement. Jump to the [Total Exclusion Zone](stratmap://map?lat=-51.8&lon=-59.0&zoom=7).

### ARA General Belgrano is sunk

**2 May 1982**

The sinking helped push Argentina's surface fleet back toward port and made the maritime contest increasingly about air attack and British fleet protection.

### British land at San Carlos Water

**21 May 1982**

The landing transformed the campaign. From this point onward the British challenge was to hold the beachhead under air attack and build a ground line of advance toward Stanley. Jump to [San Carlos Water](stratmap://map?lat=-51.566&lon=-59.08&zoom=11).

### Goose Green confirms British momentum

**28-29 May 1982**

The fight at Goose Green mattered beyond its scale. It signaled that the British could move inland, fight set-piece battles, and hold the initiative under difficult logistics. Jump to [Goose Green](stratmap://map?lat=-51.8228&lon=-58.9792&zoom=11).

### Argentine forces surrender at Stanley

**14 June 1982**

After the British advance onto the heights west of town, the campaign ended with the surrender of Argentine forces in Stanley and the reestablishment of British control.
