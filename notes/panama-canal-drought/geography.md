## Geography and map logic

The canal works because water stored in the watershed can move ships across the Isthmus. When that water balance weakens, the operational consequences appear first in draft limits and daily transit counts, then in queues and route choices.


### Gatun Lake as the real bottleneck

The lake is not background scenery. It is the freshwater battery that makes lock operations possible, so falling lake levels become falling canal capacity.


### Lock complexes as water users

Miraflores, Pedro Miguel, and the Atlantic side of the canal show how throughput and water use are inseparable.


### Atlantic and Pacific queues as market signals

Once ships begin waiting longer on either side, the canal's hydrology has become a freight and scheduling signal visible to operators worldwide.
