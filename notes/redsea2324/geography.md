## Geography and map logic

The Bab el-Mandeb compresses shipping into a narrow maritime gate between Yemen, Djibouti, and Eritrea. That compression makes surveillance, harassment, interception, and escort all highly geography-dependent.


### Bab el-Mandeb as a classic chokepoint

The strait matters because ships cannot spread out very far without losing time, fuel, or navigational confidence. Narrow water amplifies both the real threat and the psychological effect of each incident.


### The Hodeidah coast as the launch arc

Much of the relevant threat geometry starts on the Yemeni coast. Missiles, drones, and maritime seizures all become more understandable once the coastal launch arc is visible relative to the shipping lane.


### The Cape route as the decision threshold

The detour around southern Africa is not simply an alternate line on the map. It is a visible indicator that operators now value predictability and crew safety over canal transit time.
