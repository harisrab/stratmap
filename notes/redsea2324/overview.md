# Red Sea Maritime Security 2023-2024

![Bab el-Mandeb Strait between Djibouti and Yemen](/example-images/redsea2324.jpg)
*The Bab el-Mandeb Strait, the southern maritime gateway into the Red Sea.*

> A maritime security notebook focused on the Bab el-Mandeb chokepoint, missile and drone pressure from Yemen, escort missions, and the commercial rerouting decisions that followed.

## Why this notebook works

This notebook is built for readers who want to understand why a narrow strait can shake insurance markets, naval planning, and commercial schedules far beyond the Middle East. The map highlights coastal launch areas in Yemen, the shipping lane through the strait, escort activity, and the long detour around the Cape of Good Hope that carriers increasingly chose once the risk calculus changed.

Open the event sequence to see how individual attacks accumulated into a larger rerouting decision. The forces panel keeps the security actors separate from the commercial ones, which helps show that a maritime crisis is rarely only a naval story: it is also about schedules, freight rates, convoy timing, and political signaling.
