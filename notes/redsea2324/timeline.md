## Timeline and turning points

This notebook carries scenario events so you can scrub the time bar, jump between beats,
and compare how actors and geography shift together.


### Galaxy Leader seizure crystallizes the threat
**19 November 2023**

The seizure of the Galaxy Leader made the Red Sea crisis legible to a global audience. What had been a regional warning rapidly became a practical reminder that ships could be singled out and physically stopped.


### Major carriers begin broad rerouting decisions
**Mid-December 2023**

Once operators started shifting ships around the Cape, the crisis became a test of schedule resilience and inventory timing, not just of naval interception.


### Operation Prosperity Guardian is announced
**18 December 2023**

The U.S.-led mission signaled that commercial protection would be organized as a sustained multinational maritime task rather than as a series of disconnected tactical responses.


### MV Rubymar is hit and later sinks
**18 February 2024**

The attack on Rubymar underlined that the danger was not only reputational or temporary. A ship could be disabled, abandoned, and become both a safety and environmental concern.


### EU launches ASPIDES
**19 February 2024**

Europe's dedicated mission reinforced the idea that the chokepoint had become a standing freedom-of-navigation problem with separate command structures converging on the same waters.


### Attack on True Confidence brings fatalities
**6 March 2024**

The strike on True Confidence highlighted the direct human cost of continued transit under threat and reinforced the caution already shaping routing decisions.
