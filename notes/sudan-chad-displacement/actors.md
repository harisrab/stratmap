## Displaced civilians

The movement is driven by civilians fleeing fighting, repeated attacks, and the collapse of safety in parts of Darfur.


### Flight from Darfur




#### Displaced households fleeing Darfur

Families and individuals fleeing violence in Darfur moved toward the nearest available crossing points into Chad.

- Role: Core population in motion
- Geographic anchor: Flows from Darfur, especially around El Geneina, toward Adré and nearby crossings in eastern Chad.
- Why it matters: The whole map exists to show where people are coming from, where they first reach safety, and where bottlenecks form.
- What to monitor: Arrival volume, family separation, shelter needs, and whether onward relocation is available.




## Reception and aid response

Chadian authorities and international responders turned a border shock into a camp, transport, and food-security problem.


### Border reception and camps




#### Chadian reception authorities

Border and local authorities absorbed first arrivals and managed the immediate transition from crossing point to organized reception or relocation.

- Role: Border management and first reception
- Geographic anchor: Centered on Adré and other eastern Chadian entry points facing Darfur.
- Why it matters: These authorities determine whether the border strip becomes a humanitarian dead end or a functioning entry corridor.
- What to monitor: Initial registration, crowding, transport availability, and security near the border.


#### UNHCR and WFP response system

International humanitarian agencies handled camp support, relocation, shelter, food, and the coordination picture for the emergency.

- Role: Camp sustainment and relief coordination
- Geographic anchor: Spread across Adré, Farchana, and deeper camp locations in eastern Chad.
- Why it matters: They convert arrival numbers into practical questions of land, logistics, food distribution, and protection.
- What to monitor: Camp saturation, funding gaps, rainy season access, and the shift from emergency shelter to longer-term support.




## Conflict driver

The fighting in Sudan is the source pressure behind the displacement corridor even when the notebook's main operational view is humanitarian.


### Darfur conflict dynamics




#### Darfur conflict zone

Violence in and around Darfur, especially West Darfur, repeatedly drove new departures toward Chad.

- Role: Source pressure behind displacement
- Geographic anchor: Concentrated around El Geneina and surrounding areas where attacks triggered flight toward the frontier.
- Why it matters: Without the source geography, the border emergency becomes detached from the conflict that creates it.
- What to monitor: Changes in violence intensity and whether they produce fresh displacement waves.
