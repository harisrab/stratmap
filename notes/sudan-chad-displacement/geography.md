## Geography and map logic

The displacement corridor from Darfur into eastern Chad is defined by proximity. Violence in West Darfur spills quickly toward Adré and nearby crossings, but humanitarian stabilization requires deeper camp locations and a functioning road network beyond the frontier.


### El Geneina and the violence source area

The corridor begins with urban and peri-urban violence inside Darfur. That source geography matters because sudden attacks can generate near-immediate movement toward the border.


### Adré as the first shock absorber

Adré became a frontline humanitarian hinge: close enough for immediate arrival, but vulnerable to saturation because so many people reached it first.


### Camp depth inside Chad

Longer-term humanitarian stability depended on moving people farther from the border into camp systems with somewhat more space and services.
