> A displacement notebook tracing how violence in Darfur pushed civilians toward Chad, where border towns and camps became the key humanitarian geography.

## Why this notebook works

This notebook follows the displacement arc from fighting in Sudan's west toward a handful of border crossings and camps in Chad. It is useful because the crisis is often described in national terms, while the operational reality is highly local: one border town, one road, one camp cluster, and one rainy season can reshape the whole humanitarian picture.

The timeline shows how a conflict outbreak became a border emergency and then a longer-term camp and services challenge. The map helps separate the place people are fleeing from, the place they first arrive, and the place humanitarian organizations try to relocate them once the border strip becomes too crowded.
