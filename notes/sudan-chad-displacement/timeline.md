## Timeline and turning points

This notebook carries scenario events so you can scrub the time bar, jump between beats,
and compare how actors and geography shift together.


### War breaks out in Sudan
**15 April 2023**

Nationwide conflict between the Sudanese Armed Forces and the Rapid Support Forces begins, creating the conditions for large-scale displacement from Darfur into Chad.


### Violence in West Darfur drives mass flight to Adré
**May 2023**

Attacks in and around El Geneina sent large numbers of people toward Adré, rapidly turning the border area into an emergency reception site.


### Road and camp access deteriorates in the rains
**Rainy season 2023**

Seasonal weather compounded the crisis by making roads harder to use and stretching food, water, and shelter support across difficult terrain.


### Relocation deeper into Chad becomes a priority
**Late 2023**

Humanitarian planning shifted toward moving people farther from the border into camps that offered somewhat more room and security than the initial reception strip.


### Arrivals continue and camp saturation deepens
**Early 2024**

The crisis persisted into 2024, with new arrivals continuing to stress already crowded camps and service networks in eastern Chad.


### The corridor hardens into a protracted emergency
**Mid-2024**

What began as a border shock increasingly resembled a protracted displacement crisis with long-term needs around shelter, food, and protection.
