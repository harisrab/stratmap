## Geography and map logic

The Suez Canal is valuable precisely because it cuts a large geographic corner out of Asia-Europe traffic. That also means any disruption in the narrow channel immediately radiates into anchorages, schedules, and fuel calculations far beyond Egypt.


### Grounding point and constrained channel geometry

At the blockage site, there was little room for error or easy bypass. The canal's engineering efficiency also made it vulnerable to single-point failure.


### North and south queue formation

Port Said and Suez anchorage became visible indicators of systemic congestion. The queue was not merely a count of ships; it was a map of deferred schedules.


### Cape route as the fallback

The diversion around southern Africa was slower and more expensive, but it set the outer boundary of what carriers could do if the canal remained closed.
