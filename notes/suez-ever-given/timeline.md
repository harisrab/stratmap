## Timeline and turning points

This notebook carries scenario events so you can scrub the time bar, jump between beats,
and compare how actors and geography shift together.


### Ever Given grounds across the canal
**23 March 2021**

A single grounding turned the canal into a complete blockage and instantly converted a local navigation incident into a global logistics shock.


### North and south queues begin building
**24 March 2021**

Traffic waiting outside the canal turned the blockage into a visibly systemic disruption, with every delayed ship representing wider schedule and cargo effects.


### Dredging and tug operations intensify
**27-28 March 2021**

The response shifted into a race between salvage progress and the economic pressure created by waiting traffic and early rerouting decisions.


### Ever Given is refloated
**29 March 2021**

The refloating ended the physical blockage but not the operational problem. Queued ships still had to be sequenced through the canal.


### Backlog clearance begins
**31 March 2021**

Convoy sequencing and catch-up operations became the next phase of the story as operators tried to understand how much of the network shock would persist downstream.


### Ship released after compensation dispute
**7 July 2021**

The incident moved from salvage into liability, compensation, and reputational questions about who bore the cost of the disruption.
