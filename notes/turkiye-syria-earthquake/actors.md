## Türkiye response architecture

Türkiye's state and provincial response operated across a huge damage belt while also serving as the rear logistics platform for cross-border aid into Syria.


### State and logistics system




#### AFAD and provincial response

Türkiye's disaster management authority coordinated search, shelter, and logistics across multiple heavily affected provinces.

- Role: National disaster coordination
- Geographic anchor: Centered on Gaziantep, Kahramanmaraş, Hatay, and the wider southern provincial network.
- Why it matters: It linked local rescue pressure to national logistics, shelter, and cross-border coordination.
- What to monitor: Road access, shelter demand, fuel, and how quickly provincial nodes regained operational rhythm.


#### WHO and WFP logistics nodes

International logistics and health actors helped move aid through Turkish hubs and toward the most stressed areas.

- Role: Relief routing and health support
- Geographic anchor: Linked airports, warehouses, and onward road corridors in southern Türkiye to border points serving northwest Syria.
- Why it matters: Their performance determined how quickly the response could shift from rescue shock to sustained relief.
- What to monitor: Aid throughput, health facility support, and corridor reliability.




## Cross-border Syria response

Northwest Syria depended on local responders and cross-border channels that were already politically constrained before the earthquakes.


### Local and UN cross-border response




#### Syrian Civil Defence and local responders

Local search-and-rescue and community responders were among the first actors operating inside devastated Syrian communities.

- Role: Immediate local rescue and relief
- Geographic anchor: Centered in northwest Syrian towns and settlements west and south of the Turkish border crossings.
- Why it matters: They bridged the gap before larger aid volumes could move across the border.
- What to monitor: Rescue access, heavy equipment shortages, and speed of relief handoff.


#### UN cross-border relief channel

The UN cross-border mechanism was essential for moving international aid from Türkiye into northwest Syria.

- Role: Cross-border corridor management
- Geographic anchor: Focused on Bab al-Hawa first, and then on additional authorized crossings as access widened.
- Why it matters: Without these routes, stockpiles in Türkiye could not become timely relief inside Syria.
- What to monitor: Crossing status, customs and permissions, convoy scheduling, and onward distribution inside Syria.
