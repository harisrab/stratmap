## Geography and map logic

The disaster zone cut across provinces in southern Türkiye and northwest Syria, but not all damaged places were equally reachable. Relief depended on which roads and crossings remained usable and on whether aid could move from Turkish hubs toward the Syrian side quickly enough.


### The quake belt across Kahramanmaraş, Gaziantep, and Hatay

The impact zone was broad, but the operational picture became clearest when mapped around urban clusters, damaged corridors, and hospital or shelter concentration points.


### Bab al-Hawa as the core cross-border hinge

For northwest Syria, the border crossing was the practical hinge between international aid stocks in Türkiye and communities on the Syrian side.


### Iskenderun and port-area disruption

Damage and fires near Iskenderun reminded responders that a disaster corridor can also lose parts of its own logistics system just when demand spikes.
