> A humanitarian response notebook showing the quake belt, damaged cities, border crossings into northwest Syria, and the logistics corridors that mattered in the first weeks.

## Why this notebook works

This notebook focuses on relief geography rather than only damage geography. The earthquakes were catastrophic, but the operational story also depended on roads, airports, fuel access, damaged hospitals, cross-border permissions, and the handful of routes that connected Turkish logistics hubs to northwest Syria.

The event list helps show how the response changed over days rather than months: shock, rescue, infrastructure loss, border bottlenecks, and then the partial widening of aid access. The forces panel separates state authorities, UN logistics, and local Syrian responders so the human and organizational picture does not collapse into a single generic 'aid' actor.
