## Timeline and turning points

This notebook carries scenario events so you can scrub the time bar, jump between beats,
and compare how actors and geography shift together.


### Main shock strikes southern Türkiye
**6 February 2023, 04:17 local**

The first major earthquake devastated a wide belt of provinces in Türkiye and affected communities across the border in Syria, immediately overwhelming local response capacity.


### Second major quake compounds the emergency
**6 February 2023, later that day**

A second major earthquake struck hours later, widening the impact zone, damaging already stressed routes, and increasing the need for shelter and medical support.


### Roads, airports, and depots struggle to stabilize
**7 February 2023**

The response shifted from pure rescue urgency to a logistics problem as roads, fuel, hospitals, and airport operations all showed varying levels of disruption.


### Bab al-Hawa becomes the central cross-border relief hinge
**9 February 2023**

For northwest Syria, the crossing became the key geographic question: whether enough aid could move across fast enough to meet the spike in demand.


### Additional crossings are authorized
**13 February 2023**

The opening of extra border points widened the map of possible relief movement and reduced dependence on a single corridor into northwest Syria.


### Response shifts toward sustained shelter and health support
**Late February 2023**

As the rescue phase receded, the geography of the operation increasingly centered on tents, temporary shelter, damaged hospitals, and reliable onward distribution.
